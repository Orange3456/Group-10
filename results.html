Aspect

Aspect values typically range between 0 and 360 degrees and represent the horizontal direction that a slope faces. In the case of a value ranging from 5.71 to 360, it can be interpreted in two ways:

Slope

The slope values typically indicate the steepness of a terrain and are expressed as a percentage. However, in isolation, slope values ranging from 0 to 66.15 alone (without any context regarding the aspect) are difficult to interpret definitively.

DTM

Digital Terrain Models (DTM) sometimes called Digital Elevation Models (DEM) is a topographic model of the bare Earth that can be manipulated by computer programs.

NDVI

NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) values typically range from -1 to 1 and are used to assess vegetation health and biomass.

Landslide Susceptibility Map

A Landslide Susceptibility Map with values ranging from 0 to 100 indicates the relative likelihood of landslides occurring in different areas.
